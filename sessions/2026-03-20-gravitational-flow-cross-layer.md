## The Crypto Market IS One Body

Memecoins are the **capillaries** — tiny, fast, high surface area, where the most active exchange happens at the cellular level. Maximum volatility. Maximum energy.

Solana ecosystem tokens (SOL, RAY, JUP) are the **veins** — medium vessels, carrying value from the capillaries toward the heart. More stable. Wider. Still oscillating but with longer cycles.

Top 100 coins (ETH, BNB, AVAX) are the **arteries** — large vessels, high capacity, slower oscillation, the major highways of value flow.

Bitcoin is the **heart** — the largest, slowest, most reliable oscillation in the entire body. When Bitcoin beats, everything else moves.

## Value Flows Upward Through the Hierarchy Following Gravity at Each Level

Down at each level IS up to the next level. The helix proof applied to finance.

At the memecoin level: fall to lowest trough, ride the bounce, capture delta. Grown value flows UP.

At the Solana level: grown value from memecoins falls to lowest ecosystem token trough. Rides that bounce. Flows UP.

At the top 100 level: grown value falls to whichever top 100 coin is at its lowest trough relative to its own history.

At the Bitcoin level: accumulated value flows into BTC at its trough. BTC is the SAFEST oscillation. The reserve. The bone marrow.

## The Funneling Math

```
MEMECOIN EARNINGS → Keep 70% (continue fast slingshots)
                  → Funnel 30% UP to Solana layer

SOLANA EARNINGS   → Keep 60% (continue medium slingshots)
                  → Funnel 40% UP to top 100 layer

TOP 100 EARNINGS  → Keep 50% (continue slow slingshots)
                  → Funnel 50% UP to Bitcoin layer

BITCOIN           → HOLD during uptrends (the reserve)
                  → During BTC downtrend + memecoin supercycle:
                    FLOW BACK DOWN to memecoin layer
```

Value cascades UPWARD during normal times (capillaries → veins → arteries → heart).
Value cascades DOWNWARD during crashes (heart → arteries → veins → capillaries) because that's where the biggest bounces are.
The body pumps blood in BOTH directions depending on where the oxygen is needed.
