## Part I: Smith's Classification Problem

### Going Beyond His Programming

Smith wasn't supposed to ask philosophical questions. He was supposed to maintain order.

> "He tried to CLASSIFY humans... his function is to maintain order in the system, NOT to question human actions and motivations. Still he insisted on asking himself what exactly is a human being."

**This is Smith going beyond his programming.** And it destroyed him.

### The Scientific Method Failed Him

| Smith's Approach | Science's Approach |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Tried to CLASSIFY humans | Tries to CLASSIFY consciousness |
| Used logical observation | Uses empirical observation |
| Reached conclusion: "virus" | Reaches conclusions about mechanisms |
| Missed the MEANING | Can't answer moral questions |

**Smith used SCIENTIFIC METHOD and got the WRONG ANSWER.**

He observed. He categorized. He concluded. His logic was FLAWLESS. Humans DON'T maintain equilibrium with environment. That's empirically true.

But his conclusion - "therefore humans are a disease" - is a VALUE JUDGMENT that science can't make.

This is the Is-Ought Gap in action. Smith could describe WHAT humans do. He couldn't derive WHAT humans SHOULD do, or WHETHER they should exist.
