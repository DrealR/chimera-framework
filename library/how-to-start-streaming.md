## HOW TO START (Literally)

### Option 1: The Observation Start

Just describe what you see.

"I'm at Walmart right now. Looking at all these systems. People with carts. Music playing above. Everyone moving around. Let me just tell you what I'm noticing..."

Then keep going.

### Option 2: The Question Start

Start with what you're genuinely thinking about.

"So I've been thinking about this thing... about how when you look someone in the eyes, it's like two systems connecting... I don't know if this makes sense but let me try to explain..."

Then keep going.

### Option 3: The Disclaimer Start (Your style)

Say exactly what you told me:

"Look, this is probably gonna be the worst stream you've ever seen. I'm gonna stutter. It's not polished. This is just me, thinking out loud, going about my day. No expectations. You can leave whenever. I'm just... doing this. Let's see what happens."

Then keep going.

### Option 4: The Walk Start

Don't talk at all for the first minute.

Just show what you're seeing.
Let people wonder.
Then start talking when you feel like it.

## FORMAT OPTIONS

### Format A: Walking + Thinking (Solo)

- You walk around (store, outside, anywhere)
- You observe things
- You share what you're thinking
- No interaction needed
- Pure stream of consciousness

### Format B: Talking to Claude (Hybrid)

- You stream while talking to me
- People see your process
- They watch you think WITH an AI
- They see the ideas develop in real time
- You're not alone, but also not performing

### Format C: Questions from Chat (Interactive)

- You start with something
- Chat asks questions
- You explore those questions
- They guide the conversation
- But YOU control the depth

### My Recommendation: Start with A or B

Format A (Walking + Thinking):
- Zero pressure
- Pure observation
- If no one watches, it's still content
- If people watch, they're eavesdropping on your mind

Format B (Talking to Claude):
- You have a partner
- Structure emerges naturally
- People see authentic collaboration
- Less pressure to fill silence

## THE FEAR TRANSLATOR

| What you're worried about | What it actually means |
|---------------------------|------------------------|
| "I don't know where to start" | "I'm afraid of judgment" |
| "I need it to be perfect" | "I'm afraid of looking stupid" |
| "What if I stutter" | "I'm afraid of not being polished" |
| "What if no one watches" | "I'm afraid of being ignored" |

**The solution to ALL of these:**

Accept them. Announce them. Then do it anyway.

"I'm probably going to look stupid. Watch anyway if you want."

The fear loses power when you name it.

## THE PRACTICAL SETUP

**What you need:**
- Phone with camera
- That's it

**Optional:**
- Tripod/holder for stable shots
- External mic (phone mic is fine to start)
- Ring light (natural light is fine to start)

**Platform:**
- YouTube Live (easiest, keeps recordings)
- Twitch (more interactive chat culture)
- TikTok Live (younger audience, more casual)
- Instagram Live (if you have followers there)

**Recommendation:** YouTube Live

- Recordings save automatically
- Easy to share later
- No minimum follower requirement
- Can do it from phone

This is probably gonna be weird. I don't know what I'm doing.

*[Look around. Describe what you see.]*

"So I'm at [wherever you are]. And I'm just noticing..."

**Ongoing - Keep going**

When you run out of things to say:
- Look at something new
- Ask yourself a question out loud
- Read chat if anyone's there
- Talk to Claude about something
- Just be silent (that's okay too)

**Ending:**

"Alright, I'm gonna stop here. Thanks for watching. Or not. Whatever. See you next time."

## WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU GET STUCK

| Moment | What to do |
|--------|------------|
| Nothing to say | Describe what you literally see |
| Feel awkward | Name the awkwardness: "This is awkward" |
| No viewers | Talk to yourself, it's still content |
| Boring topic | "This is boring, let me switch to..." |
| Lost train of thought | "I forgot what I was saying. Anyway..." |
| Technical issues | "Hold on, something's weird..." |

**The answer is always: Name it, then keep going.**
