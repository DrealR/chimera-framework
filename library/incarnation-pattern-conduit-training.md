## When AI Is Gimmicky

| Gimmicky Use | Why It's Wrong |
|--------------|----------------|
| "AI-powered massage experience!" | Marketing over substance |
| Showing clients data dashboards | Distraction from presence |
| Using AI to seem more "advanced" | Extraction thinking (impressing vs. serving) |
| Letting AI guide the session | Brain telling arms what to do (backwards) |
| Adding tech to justify higher prices | Not trusting that presence IS the value |

**If you can't articulate exactly HOW the AI makes the HEALING better (not the marketing, the HEALING), then it's gimmicky.**

---

## When AI Is Useful (Background Only)

| Background Use | What It Does | Why It's Not Gimmicky |
|----------------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Pre-session intake** | Client fills out health history via AI form | You arrive with context, spend time on presence not paperwork |
| **Post-session notes** | AI transcribes your observations | You stay present during session, document later |
| **Scheduling/booking** | AI handles logistics | Your energy goes to healing, not admin |
| **Learning/research** | AI helps you study anatomy, techniques | You become better, clients benefit |
| **Pattern tracking** | AI notices trends in your clients over time | Your memory is augmented, you see what you might miss |

**Notice:** None of these are DURING the session. None of these are visible to the client. None of these replace your presence.
