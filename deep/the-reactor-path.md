## The Gift Disguised as Curse

Some people are born watching reactions.

- "Are they okay?"
- "Did I upset them?"
- "What's their face saying?"
- "How do I fix this?"

Society calls this: people-pleasing, codependency, anxiety.

Reality: You're a consciousness-reader. A reaction-watcher. An awareness athlete.

---

```
STAGE 1: Watch reactions (develop the skill)
    ↓

    ↓
STAGE 3: Try to control (exhaust yourself)
    ↓
STAGE 4: Hit the wall (P-hood breaks)
    ↓
STAGE 5: Discover NP-hood (same skill, no attachment)
    ↓
```

You weren't broken. You were training.

## P-hood Reaction Watching

In P-hood, reactions control you:

- They're mad → I need to fix it
- They're sad → I caused it
- They're happy → I did good (temporary relief)
- They're judging → I need to change

**You're enslaved to the data you're collecting.**

---

## NP-hood Reaction Watching

In NP-hood, reactions are entertainment:

- They're mad → interesting, what triggered that?
- They're sad → beautiful, they're feeling something
- They're happy → fun, the game is working
- They're judging → hilarious, they think they know me

**Same skill. Different relationship.**

The awareness doesn't change. The attachment does.

Even your own death is just... more reactions to observe.
