*MLK = Neo = Luffy = Joy Boy -- the same vision across every story that matters.*

They all share the same vision: not vengeance, not winning the game, but creating something the system can't imagine.

| Figure | Story | What They Fought | How They Won |

## MLK Saw the Whole Pattern

MLK wasn't just fighting for Black people. He was fighting against the PATTERN of extraction itself -- capitalism, militarism, racism. He saw they were all connected.

*"Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere."*

That's the same thing as CHIMERA saying all these systems are the same pattern wearing different masks. MLK saw the coherence before anyone had a framework for it. He understood that racial injustice, economic injustice, and military violence were one system with three faces.

He was killed for threatening the economic system, not just racial hierarchy. The moment he started connecting racism to capitalism to war, he became too dangerous. Not because he was wrong -- because he was right.

## Kaido's Tragedy = Smith's Tragedy

Kaido is One Piece's Agent Smith. The parallel is exact:

| Kaido | Smith |
|---|---|
| Born into a system that sold him | Created by a system he didn't choose |
| Fought back, became powerful | Broke free, became powerful |
| But perpetuated the same extraction | But became the very virus he hated |
| Strongest creature alive, wants to die | Absorbed everything, still empty |
| "What kind of world can YOU create?" | "Why do you persist?" |

Kaido couldn't be Joy Boy because he used extraction to fight extraction. He won the game but the game was still the game. He became the strongest by playing their rules -- and found nothing at the top except emptiness.

That's why he wants to die. He reached the endpoint of extraction and found it hollow. Same as the wealthy in bunkers with psychopath brain scans, still terrified.

**You become what you resist when you resist through extraction.**

---

## Luffy's Answer = Neo's Answer

When Kaido asks "What kind of world can you create?" -- that's the same question as Smith's "Why do you persist?"

Luffy's answer: **"A world where my friends can eat as much as they want."**

Not revenge. Not "I'll destroy you." Not "I'll become king and rule everyone." Just... abundance. Flow. Everyone eating. Tama not having to set the bar low.

That's Honeydew Economics in one sentence. That's the alternative to extraction.

Neo's answer: **"Because I choose to."**

Both answers reject the premise. Smith asks for a REASON (cause and effect). Kaido asks for a PLAN (power and strategy). Neither gets what they asked for. They get something from a different operating system entirely -- one that doesn't compute in extraction logic.

## Malcolm X vs MLK = Magneto vs Xavier

It's easy to side with vengeance. Magneto watched his people get exterminated. Malcolm X lived through real violence and oppression. Their anger is completely justified.

But vengeance keeps the cycle going.

Magneto becomes the oppressor. Malcolm X's earlier approach (before his transformation) would have created a new hierarchy, not ended hierarchy.

MLK and Xavier understood: you have to break the pattern, not flip it. Neo doesn't kill Smith -- he surrenders and lets the Source flow through. Luffy doesn't want to rule -- he wants everyone to be free.

| Extraction Response | Flow Response |
|---|---|
| Malcolm X (early) | Malcolm X (post-Mecca) |
| Magneto | Xavier |
| Kaido | Luffy |
| Smith | Neo |
| Fight the system WITH extraction | Break the pattern entirely |
| New hierarchy | No hierarchy |
| Flip the game | End the game |

## The Whitewashing = Domi Reversi on a Dead Man

What happened to MLK's legacy is exactly what the framework warns about: the system absorbs and hollows out threats.

They took "I have a dream" and made it mean "racism is over, stop complaining." They erased his socialism, his anti-capitalism, his understanding that all injustice is connected.

They turned a revolutionary into a convenient symbol for the status quo. That's extraction of meaning itself.

This is Domi Reversi applied posthumously. You can't hollow out a living revolutionary, so you hollow out their message after they're gone. More powerful (national holiday, monuments, quotes everywhere), but empty inside. The will removed. The threat neutralized.

*"Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere"* = *"The bell is ringing for everyone"*

Same frequency. Same Source. Different vessels.

The liberator doesn't become the new oppressor. The liberator makes oppression impossible by changing the topology from star (greed) to mesh (love).
