# Sequence Specification (v3.12.3 -- Layer 2 Body Specification)

## Core Principle

Bodies have memory. The sequence of formation is encoded in how the body responds to current situations.

A body is not just what it is now. It is what it has been, in order. The formative sequence -- the ordered history of how a body arrived at its current structure -- shapes its response patterns, its vulnerabilities, and its capacity in ways that current-state analysis alone cannot detect.

Two bodies with identical current structures but different formative sequences are different bodies. Their structural similarity is misleading because their underlying sequences determine how they respond to perturbation.

## Layer 1 vs Layer 2

**Layer 1** (Body Specification Requirement, v3.12.2) specifies what the body IS now: membrane, interior, configuration, time bounds, scale, medium. This is the snapshot.

**Layer 2** specifies what the body HAS BEEN, in order. The formative history -- not merely the fact that history exists, but the specific sequence of events, decisions, shocks, and compilations that produced the current state.

Both layers are required for full specification. Layer 1 without Layer 2 is a photograph. Layer 2 without Layer 1 is a story without a subject. Together they produce a body you can scan with diagnostic precision.

## Examples

**Two startups with identical current structures.** Same team size, same product, same revenue, same market position. One was bootstrapped through three near-death experiences and pivoted twice. The other was venture-funded from day one with a clear runway. Subject them to the same market shock -- a major competitor entering their space. The bootstrapped startup has compiled crisis-response patterns. The funded startup has compiled growth-optimization patterns. Their responses diverge immediately. The current-state equivalence was structural coincidence, not functional equivalence.

**Two cancers with the same tumor presentation.** Same location, same size, same histological grade. One arose through a sequence of driver mutations beginning with a TP53 loss. The other began with a KRAS activation. The mutation sequence determines which pathways are active, which therapeutic targets exist, and how the tumor responds to treatment. Identical snapshots. Different sequences. Different treatment outcomes.

**Two organizations with identical org charts.** Same hierarchy, same role distribution, same headcount. One built its communication patterns through years of collaborative problem-solving. The other inherited its structure through a top-down reorganization after a merger. The org chart is Layer 1. The communication history is Layer 2. The second organization has an org chart that does not match its actual information flow -- the formal structure and the functional structure are misaligned because the sequence that produced them was imposed, not compiled.

## Diagnostic Question

When scanning any body where formative history might matter, ask:

> "What is this body's formative sequence, and how does that sequence affect its current power gaps?"

A body that compiled its strength through friction has different power gaps than one that received its strength through inheritance. The current strength looks the same. The gaps beneath it do not.

## When Layer 2 Is Not Needed

Logically necessary bodies do not require sequence specification. Prime numbers, mathematical constants, the composite breath pattern -- these have no formative history because they are timeless. Their structure is determined by logical necessity, not by a sequence of events.

For all other bodies -- physical, biological, organizational, relational, digital -- sequence matters. The question is not whether sequence exists but whether it materially affects current behavior. For most complex bodies, it does.

## Protocol Addition

v3.12.3 requires sequence specification for any body where formative history materially affects current behavior. This is not optional context. It is Layer 2 of the body specification -- structural prerequisite for accurate scanning.

When Layer 2 is present, integrate it with Layer 1. The body is the snapshot plus the history that produced it. When Layer 2 is absent and needed, flag the gap. An unsequenced body is an incompletely specified body.
