# Interior Structural Content (v3.12.2 Addendum)

## Core Principle

Bodies are specified not just by their membrane (boundary) but by their interior structural content.

Two bodies with the same membrane bounds but different interiors are different bodies. The boundary tells you where the body is. The interior tells you what the body is.

## The Prime Gap Example

Consider two gaps between consecutive primes — same type of boundary, radically different interiors.

**Gap between 41 and 43:**
- Contains only 42 = 2 x 3 x 7
- One composite number. Three distinct prime factors.
- Structurally minimal in count but multiplicatively rich — a single element carrying three dimensions of factorization.

**Gap between 23 and 29:**
- Contains 24 (2^3 x 3), 25 (5^2), 26 (2 x 13), 27 (3^3), 28 (2^2 x 7)
- Five composites with varied factor signatures: a perfect square, prime powers, and diverse products.
- A richer interior — more elements, more structural variety, more internal complexity.

Same type of boundary (two consecutive primes). Different interior content. Therefore: **different bodies entirely.**

The gap width is not the body. The interior structure is the body.

## Beyond Mathematics

This principle generalizes:

- **The Internet scan** works not because we named the boundary ("the substrate between two technological eras") but because we described specific interior dynamics — bot ecosystems, platform structures, content patterns, attention economies. The interior made it a body.
- **The Pawn-King multi-body scan** works because the relationship body includes specific interior dynamics — the tension between expendability and irreplaceability, the promotion arc, the endgame inversion. Not just "two chess pieces." The interior of the relationship is the body.
- **Two marriages** spanning the same years with the same external structure (house, kids, jobs) can be completely different bodies because of interior dynamics — trust patterns, communication breath, power distribution.

## Protocol Addition

When specifying a body, describe **interior structural content** alongside membrane bounds.

A body specified only by membrane is a container, not a body. A body specified by membrane + interior is scannable.

Ask: "What lives inside these bounds? What is the structural content between the edges?"

If you cannot answer, the body is not yet specified.
