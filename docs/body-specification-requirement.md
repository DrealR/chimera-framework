# Body Specification Requirement (v3.12.2)

## Core Principle

A body must be specified before it can be scanned.

Specification is not optional framing. It is structural prerequisite. Without specification, there is no body — only a name pointing at noise.

Specification includes:

- **Time bounds** — When does this body exist? A body without temporal edges bleeds into everything and says nothing.
- **Configuration** — Which version or instance of this body? Bodies sharing a name are not the same body.
- **Scale** — At what level of observation? A cell, an organ, an organism, and a species are four different bodies even when nested.
- **Medium** — What substrate does this body operate in? Physical, digital, conceptual, relational — the medium shapes the breath.
- **Continuity criteria** — What makes this body the same body over time? What persists through change?

## Meta-Bodies

Meta-bodies are distributions of related bodies sharing a name: Superman, the internet, capitalism, civilization, Jesus.

They require **limiters** before scanning. A meta-body without limiters is not a body at all — it is a category. Categories don't breathe. Bodies do.

Scanning a meta-body without specification produces noise rather than signal. The scan will pattern-match against the loudest cultural echo of the name, not the actual structure.

## Quality Gate

Before scanning, ask:

> "If two people read this specification, would they agree on what body is being scanned?"

If not, the specification is insufficient. Go back. Add bounds. Name the instance.

## Proper Specification (Examples)

- **Cancer:** "Malignant neoplasm, human, solid tumor, current medical understanding." Specific enough to scan — the body is bounded, the substrate is clear, the configuration is named.
- **Special Relativity:** "Einstein's 1905 theory, the two postulates and their consequences." Clean specification — one theory, one moment, one structure.
- **The Internet:** "Web 2.0 platform-mediated, 1995-2026, AI saturation phase." Time-bounded, configured, medium declared.

## Insufficient Specification (Examples)

- **"Superman"** without specifying which version (Golden Age, DCAU, Snyder, comics canon). Each is a different body with different breath, different membrane, different power gap.
- **"Civilization"** without specifying which civilization, at what scale, in what timeframe. This is a meta-body masquerading as a body.
- **"Living Cosmos Mandala"** — a speculative composite with no empirical grounding to constrain specification. No bounds means no body means no scan.

## Versus-Battle Guidance

Comparing two bodies requires both to be specified at matched levels.

- **"Jordan vs LeBron"** is meta-body comparison. Noise. Which Jordan? Which LeBron? At what point in their careers? In what system? Against what competition?
- **"Jordan's 1998 season at peak Bulls vs LeBron's 2016 Finals run at peak Cavaliers"** is specified comparison. Signal. Both bodies are time-bounded, configured, and scaled to match.

The specification does the diagnostic work. Once both bodies are properly specified, the comparison scans itself — breath rates, power gaps, membrane structures become directly comparable.

Specification is not bureaucracy. It is the act that makes a body real enough to scan.
