# Trust Fields and Boundary Pressure

Trust is not only a social concept; it behaves like a field variable that stabilizes or destabilizes loops.

## Core Claim

A loop persists when trust in its next step is high enough to reduce perceived uncertainty.

## Trust Loop Formation

```text
Exposure -> Repetition -> Pattern Recognition -> Trust -> Loop -> Defense
```

## Loop Strength

```text
Loop Strength = Repetitions x Emotional Intensity x Time
```

High-strength loops resist interruption. They can be liberating or extractive.

## Three Trust Polarities

1. Dark trust
- Consistent harm still builds predictability.
- Predictability can feel safer than uncertainty.
- Produces trap loops (trauma-bond style dynamics).

2. Neutral trust
- Stable expectation without moral direction.
- Useful for baseline coordination.

3. Light trust
- Consistent care plus agency.
- Builds open, adaptive loops.

## Pressure as Boundary Physics

Pressure appears at boundaries between incompatible bodies (contexts, identities, expectations), not inside a fully matched medium.

- Matched medium: low perceived pressure.
- Mismatch pocket: rising pressure and friction.
- Repeated boundary crossing with adaptation: intelligence growth.

## Spotlight and Field Attention

1. Spotlight attention
- Directed lock.
- High precision, high vulnerability to interruption.

2. Field attention
- Ambient awareness.
- Lower lock pressure, higher adaptability.

Practical principle: intervention quality depends on timing in the gap between spotlight locks.

## Disruption Rule

Directly forcing against an active loop usually increases friction and reinforces defense.

Better strategy:
1. Preserve agency.
2. Introduce adjacent evidence.
3. Repeat low-force exposures until trust re-anchors.

## Coherent vs Incoherent External Pressure

External pressure is strongest when belief and desire align against a target.

- Coherent pressure: belief and desire aligned -> stronger suppression.
- Incoherent pressure: belief and desire conflict -> weaker suppression.

This distinction explains why some agents outperform hostile environments once repetition density is high.

## Engineering Translation

1. Architecture
- Prefer riverbeds (data-shaped flow) over dams (branch-heavy forcing).

2. State
- Preserve event history to observe trajectory, not only snapshot state.

3. Control
- Convert lock-breaking moments into pause-aware routing decisions.

4. Product
- Build for open-barrier transitions: guide users through boundaries without coercive pressure.

## Relationship Translation

- Consistency builds trust faster than image management.
- Mirroring others' latency for control erodes trust coherence.
- Name needs directly; do not force outcomes.

## Economic Translation

Money functions as a large-scale trust proxy among weakly connected participants.

When local relational trust rises, direct exchange and mutual support require less synthetic trust infrastructure.

## Protocol Summary

1. Detect active loop and its trust anchor.
2. Classify pressure as coherent/incoherent.
3. Introduce low-force adjacent evidence.
4. Repeat exposures to seed new trust.
5. Track trajectory with immutable history.
6. Convert circle loops to spiral loops via pause + agency.
