## Key Findings

1. **The gap is systematic, not random.** The Partial classification dominates because Question 9 (hysteresis) asks "what scars remain?" while Layer 2 asks "what was the ordered sequence of formation, and how does each stage determine current behavior?" Scans that answered Q9 well still did not meet Layer 2.

2. **Biology naturally includes Layer 2.** Cancer and Neuron both treat formative sequence as structural specification because sequence IS the body in biology. Other domains treat history as context, not specification.

3. **Remediation effort is moderate.** Most Partial scans contain enough historical material to anchor a Layer 2 section — they need reorganization and specification of order, not research from scratch. The two Absent scans (chess pieces) require new content about chess history.

4. **This assessment covers 15 of 82 scans.** Extrapolating: the majority of the corpus is likely Partial or Absent against Layer 2. A full-corpus assessment would confirm the scale.
