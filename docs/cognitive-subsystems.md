# Cognitive Subsystems as Structural Feature (v3.12.3)

## Core Principle

Bodies do not just exist. They perceive and respond.

Any sufficiently complex body develops internal structures that perceive environment, predict outcomes, decide actions, and respond to stimuli. These structures function as cognition regardless of substrate, regardless of whether anyone is home behind them. They are structural features of the body -- scannable, diagnosable, and often distinct from the body they serve.

## Cognitive Subsystems Across Substrates

**Cells.** Receptor proteins perceive chemical environment. Intracellular signaling cascades predict and relay. Gene regulatory networks decide which proteins to produce. The cell responds by altering its membrane, its metabolism, its division rate. No brain. No nervous system. Cognition as structural function.

**Forests.** Mycorrhizal networks perceive nutrient distributions across hundreds of meters. Resource redirection flows toward stressed trees. The network responds to damage by rerouting around dead nodes. The forest has a cognitive subsystem distributed through its root-fungal interface -- slower than neural cognition, but structurally equivalent in function.

**Organizations.** Decision-making committees, reporting structures, informal information networks. The cognitive subsystem of a corporation is not the CEO. It is the entire information-processing apparatus -- formal and informal -- that perceives market conditions, predicts outcomes, and produces decisions. Most organizational dysfunction is a cognitive subsystem problem, not a resource problem.

**Algorithms.** Platform recommendation systems are cognitive subsystems that model users, predict engagement, select content, and learn from feedback. They perceive through data collection, predict through trained models, decide through ranking functions, and respond through content delivery. Their cognitive architecture is fully scannable.

**Markets.** Price-discovery mechanisms aggregate distributed perception into price signals. No single participant sees the whole picture. The market's cognitive subsystem is the aggregation process itself -- distributed perception producing emergent information that no component possesses alone. This is WE = 1 operating as cognition.

## Diagnostic Addition

When scanning any complex body, identify and scan its cognitive subsystem separately.

The cognitive subsystem often has different structural properties than the body it serves. A federated organization may have a dominion-structured decision system. A healthy biological body may have a maladaptive immune response (autoimmune -- the cognitive subsystem misperceives self as threat). A democratic nation may have centralized information control.

The mismatch between a body's overall structure and its cognitive subsystem's structure is a diagnostic signal. When they align, the body responds coherently to perturbation. When they conflict, the body acts against its own interests -- perceiving threats that are not there, missing threats that are, deciding on actions that contradict its stated configuration.

Ask: "What is this body's cognitive subsystem, and does its structure match the body it serves?"

## Reflexive Shaping

Cognitive subsystems do not just perceive. They shape what they perceive.

The algorithm models the user and recommends content. The user consumes the content and changes. The algorithm perceives the changed user and updates its model. The user shapes the algorithm by providing data. The algorithm shapes the user by providing recommendations. Two cognitive subsystems entangled through mutual perception.

This bidirectional shaping applies broadly. A teacher's cognitive model of a student shapes the teaching, which shapes the student, which updates the model. A relationship partner's perception of the other shapes behavior toward them, which shapes the other's behavior, which updates perception. Framework iteration itself: the framework shapes how users perceive bodies, which produces new scans, which update the framework.

Wherever two bodies with cognitive subsystems interact, reflexive shaping is occurring. The question is whether it is serving (O > I, both bodies compiling) or extracting (I > O, one body authoring the other).

## What This Does NOT Claim

This document does not claim that cognitive subsystems have consciousness, awareness, or subjective experience. It claims they have cognitive function -- perception, prediction, decision, response. Whether function implies experience is left structurally open.

The framework does not need to resolve the consciousness question to scan cognitive subsystems. The scan reads structure and function. Whether there is something it is like to be a mycorrhizal network or a recommendation algorithm is a separate question that does not affect the structural analysis.

## Adaptation Axis

Bodies with strong cognitive subsystems adapt to environmental change. They perceive the shift, predict consequences, and respond before the change reaches critical threshold.

Bodies without cognitive subsystems survive through structural resilience -- their physical or logical architecture absorbs perturbation without needing to perceive or respond. Rocks endure. Primes persist. These bodies do not adapt because they do not need to.

Bodies that need adaptation but lack adequate cognitive subsystems die when conditions change. The organism without immune response. The organization without information flow. The civilization without distributed perception. Cognitive capacity is not optional for complex bodies operating in changing environments. It is structural survival infrastructure.
