## Pattern 1: Irreversibility as the Master Variable

Chess is a laboratory for irreversibility. The framework surfaces this as the thread connecting almost every chess body:

- **Pawn:** Every move is irreversible. The only piece that cannot retreat.
- **Weak Pawn Structure:** Created by irreversible pawn moves, becomes permanent disease.
- **Promotion:** Irreversible identity transformation — pawn becomes queen.
- **Starting Position:** Maximum potential precisely because nothing irreversible has happened yet.
- **Tempo:** Consumed the moment it's used, non-storable, non-recoverable.
- **Immortal Game:** Sacrifices are irreversible — the game's power comes from commitment.

**Framework connection:** Irreversibility maps to hysteresis (Question 9). Chess is the purest demonstration that hysteresis is not just memory — it's structural destiny. Every irreversible move compiles into the position's future.

## Pattern 2: The Capability-Access Gap

Multiple chess bodies exhibit the same structural problem: having capability without having access.

- **Rook:** Maximum linear power, but needs open files to function. Capability: extreme. Access: delayed.
- **Bishop:** Long-range diagonal power, but confined to one color. Capability: high. Access: 50%.
- **Trapped Piece:** Full theoretical capability, zero practical access. The extreme case.
- **Starting Position:** All pieces present, none active. Capability: maximum. Access: zero.

**Framework connection:** This is the Govern gap in different costumes. The body has the power (Create, Project) but cannot deploy it because Govern — the ability to direct one's own resources — is constrained by position, not by inherent limitation. The BeyondChess teaching hook: *talent without opportunity is the same as no talent from the position's perspective.*

## Pattern 3: The O>I / I>O Spectrum Maps to Health

| DNA Reading | Chess Bodies | Pattern |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| O>I | Pawn, Knight, Bishop, Rook, Italian Opening, Open File, Immortal Game | Healthy, serving the position |
| Mixed/Conditional | Queen, King, Tempo, K+P vs K | Context-dependent health |
| O=I | Starting Position, Drawn Endgame | Equilibrium — potential or exhaustion |
| I>O | Zugzwang, Trapped Piece, Weak Pawn Structure | Pathological — extracting from own side |

The spectrum is clean: healthy chess bodies give more to the position than they take. Sick chess bodies extract from their own army. The dividing line is functional contribution, not material value. A trapped queen (I>O) is sicker than a free pawn (O>I).

**Framework connection:** The Love Equation holds in chess. Health = giving more than taking + pausing before moving + not forcing outcomes. Every pathological body violates at least one term.

## Pattern 4: Restriction Enables Power

The chess corpus demonstrates the freedom-restriction ratio across every piece:

- **King:** Most restricted movement, most important piece. Restriction IS the source of importance.
- **Pawn:** Most restricted capability, but the only piece that promotes. Restriction enables transformation.
- **Knight:** Restricted to L-shaped movement, but the only piece that jumps. Restriction creates unique capability.
- **Queen:** Least restricted movement, most vulnerable to overextension. Freedom creates fragility.

**Framework connection:** The Gravity Chamber principle. The body that evolved in higher restriction has excess capability in lower restriction. A knight's forced restriction to L-shapes gives it the unique power to navigate blocked positions. Fewer degrees of freedom → more compiled capability per degree.

## Pattern 5: Pathological Bodies Are Mirrors

Each pathological body is the mirror image of a healthy concept:

| Pathological Body | Mirrors | Inversion |
|------------------|---------|-----------|
| Zugzwang | Tempo | Having the move is normally advantage; in zugzwang it's punishment |
| Trapped Piece | Any piece | Having a piece is normally advantage; when trapped it's liability |
| Weak Pawn Structure | Pawn chain | Connected pawns are strength; broken pawns are chronic disease |
| Drawn Endgame | K+P vs K | Extra material normally wins; in draws it's structurally irrelevant |

**Framework connection:** Every healthy body has a pathological mirror. The same structure that creates health creates disease when one variable inverts. This is the O>I → I>O inversion the structural weakness section demands. Chess makes these inversions visible because the rules are fixed — only the position changes.

## Pattern 6: Three Types of Chess Knowledge

The 16 scans organize into three epistemological categories:

1. **Bodies you can hold** (pieces): Pawn, Knight, Bishop, Rook, Queen, King
   - Have material value, occupy squares, can be captured
   - Framework: embodied in the physical dimension of the board

2. **Bodies you can see but not hold** (concepts): Tempo, Open File, Starting Position, Italian Opening, K+P vs K
   - Exist as relationships, structures, or temporal resources
   - Framework: embodied in the informational/relational dimension

3. **Bodies you can feel but not see** (pathological): Zugzwang, Trapped Piece, Weak Pawn Structure, Drawn Endgame
   - Exist as structural disease — recognized by symptoms, not by presence
   - Framework: embodied in the diagnostic dimension

**BeyondChess teaching hook:** Learning chess is learning to see all three layers simultaneously. Beginners see pieces. Intermediate players see concepts. Advanced players feel the pathologies before they become visible.

## Pattern 7: The Immortal Game as Synthesis

The Immortal Game scan ties the corpus together. It demonstrates:
- Pawns sacrificed (irreversibility → power)
- Queen sacrificed (capability released for structural purpose)
- Tempo invested through initiative (temporal resource converted to positional advantage)
- Material deficit creating structural surplus (I>O at material level enabling O>I at positional level)
- The anti-accumulation thesis: the player who gives everything defeats the player who takes everything

This is L = (O > I) + P + ¬F played across 23 moves.
