## RELATIONAL TOPOLOGY

These three bodies have never interacted in the real world. They exist in entirely separate domains. The composite is a comparative construct — not "what happens when they meet" but "what structure do they share?"

**Shared DNA:** All three are I>O. All three are dominion-configured. All three are pathological. This is the entry point. The question is what else they share that the individual scans couldn't surface.

**Cancer ↔ Memory Leak:** Both grow by accumulating what should be released. Cancer replicates cells the organism should apoptose. Memory leaks retain references the program should garbage-collect. In both cases, the pathology is a failure of Release — the body's exhale mechanism is broken. The growth looks like health (more cells! more memory!) until it overwhelms the host.

**Memory Leak ↔ Zugzwang:** Both produce paralysis through accumulation. The memory leak fills available resources until the system freezes. Zugzwang fills the position with awareness until every move is a loss. In both cases, more information makes the situation worse. Perception without Release is a trap.

**Zugzwang ↔ Cancer:** Both weaponize the system's own rules. Cancer uses the cell's native signaling to override governance. Zugzwang uses the game's compulsory-move rule to turn agency into self-harm. In both cases, the pathology is not a foreign attack — it is the system's own logic turned against itself.

**Entrainment:** None. These bodies do not entrain each other because they exist in separate dimensions. But their structural rhythms are strikingly similar: slow accumulation → invisible threshold → sudden collapse. The tempo of pathology is the same across domains.

**Federation or Dominion:** Pure dominion. All three components are dominion-configured. The composite is therefore a dominion cluster — the first in the corpus. Every prior multi-body experiment (Grok's thought experiments) produced federation composites. This is the necessary counterexample.

## STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS

1. **Selection bias in component choice.** All three components are I>O dominion bodies. The composite's findings (pathology = governance failure) may be an artifact of choosing three bodies that already share this structure. A control experiment — clustering one pathological body with two healthy bodies — would test whether the finding holds or is circular.
2. **The "failure to exhale" finding may be tautological.** I>O means intake exceeds output. Saying "pathology is the failure to exhale" may just be restating the I>O reading in different words. The non-trivial finding is the camouflage mechanism — that all three use the host's own language — which is not derivable from the I>O classification alone.
3. **Inversion condition:** The composite's finding inverts if you find a pathological body that IS O>I. A virus that kills by giving too much (cytokine storm? autoimmune response?) would break the "pathology = I>O" claim and force refinement.
