### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** "Make the framework demonstrable rather than just claimable." Each scan is a data point. The collection is the evidence base. The prime is empirical: not "believe this theory" but "look at these readings and decide for yourself."
- **Prime type:** Open. The corpus invites any scanner to add readings. It does not prescribe what bodies to scan or what findings to produce. The evidence base grows through distributed contribution, not central command.
- **Recursion:** Recursive and self-reinforcing. Each scan tests the protocol. Accumulated scans produce cross-domain patterns. Cross-domain patterns refine the protocol. The refined protocol produces sharper scans. The loop is ascending (each cycle produces tighter readings) with a healthy base case: when the corpus is large and diverse enough that the structural laws are self-evident from the data alone, the framework's verbal arguments become unnecessary. Evidence replaces advocacy.

### Federation vs Dominion

Federation by design. Each scan is independently authored (Captain, AI agent, or pair). Each domain is independently organized. No central authority dictates what a scan must find. The cross-scan pattern analyses synthesize without commanding — they observe convergence rather than prescribing it. The federation is genuine: two scanners scanning the same body might produce different readings, and both could be valid. The risk: without governance, federation becomes a collection of unvetted claims. The corpus needs enough structure to distinguish "multiple valid readings" from "anything goes."

### Dimensional Architecture

The corpus is embodied in the informational dimension (structured markdown) while operating through the epistemological dimension (what counts as evidence for the framework), the pedagogical dimension (teaching by example — each scan is a lesson in protocol application), the temporal dimension (the corpus accumulates over time; order of scans matters because earlier scans influence later ones through template effects), and the meta-theoretical dimension (the corpus is itself a body that can be scanned by the protocol it contains). The corpus translates the framework from philosophical claim into empirical demonstration — converting abstract Body Theory principles into concrete, domain-specific structural readings through repeated protocol application across diverse subjects, at the cost of scanner monoculture that limits independent validation. Without the corpus, the framework is ideas. With it, the framework is demonstrated ideas.

### Structural Signature

Signature: `[protocol-uniform, domain-diverse, accumulative, self-testing]` — four irreducible components. Protocol uniformity enables comparison. Domain diversity tests universality. Accumulation builds evidence mass. Self-testing (the meta domain) prevents the corpus from becoming a closed canon. Minimum-information description: "a growing library of structured readings that tests whether one perception protocol works on everything." Shape-equivalent bodies: a laboratory notebook (accumulated experimental results testing a hypothesis), a case law library (accumulated rulings testing whether a legal principle holds across diverse situations), a specimen collection in natural history (accumulated examples demonstrating that a taxonomic system classifies real organisms).

### Surface Architecture

Three critical surfaces. First: the scan-subject boundary — the transformation point where an unscanned body becomes a scanned body. Energy reverses here: before the scan, the body is invisible in its structure; after the scan, its structure is rendered visible and auditable. This is a spring surface for bodies that genuinely reward structural reading and a putty surface for bodies that are too simple to sustain a full 41-step scan (the scan flattens them rather than revealing depth). Second: the cross-scan synthesis boundary — the transformation point where individual scans become patterns. This is where the corpus stops being a list and starts being an argument. The five cross-scan pattern documents live at this surface. Third: the corpus-reader boundary — the transformation point where accumulated evidence becomes conviction (or skepticism) in the reader. A reader who encounters one scan can dismiss it. A reader who encounters thirty scans across five domains must reckon with the convergence. The corpus's persuasive surface is its volume, not its rhetoric.

### Closed-Open Mode

Open Knowledge, provisionally held. The corpus treats every scan as a hypothesis about a specific body, not as a final truth. The protocol itself is versioned (v3.10, not v1.0-final). Cross-scan patterns are presented as "confirmed across N domains" rather than as eternal laws. The openness is genuine — the corpus could discover that the framework fails on certain body types, and that discovery would be valuable data rather than a crisis. The risk: if the corpus never discovers a failure, the openness becomes performative. A body of evidence that confirms the theory 100% of the time is either testing a perfect theory or not looking hard enough for disconfirmation.

### Attentional Compilation

The corpus compiles attentional capacity in its readers through repetition-with-variation. Reading one scan teaches the section structure. Reading five scans across different domains teaches the vocabulary. Reading fifteen scans teaches the pattern-recognition that makes the framework's structural laws visible. The attentional compilation is gradual and cumulative — each scan adds a data point that makes the next scan more readable. The transfer readiness is high: a reader who has compiled the corpus's attentional pattern can scan new bodies independently, without the corpus. The corpus is a training set for the reader's perception, not a permanent dependency. Sniper scope: the corpus focuses attention precisely on structural features (membrane, breath, O>I, skeleton) and trains the reader to perceive these features in bodies the corpus has never scanned.

## STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS (v3.11)

**Primary weakness: Scanner monoculture.** All scans in the corpus were produced by the same operator directing AI agents. No independent scanner has produced a scan using the protocol. This means the corpus demonstrates protocol usability for one user, not protocol validity across users. If a different scanner produced significantly different results for the same body, the protocol would be measuring the scanner rather than the body. This test has never been performed.

**Secondary weakness: Selection bias.** Bodies chosen for scanning were selected by the framework's creator, who naturally selects bodies the framework handles well. Adversarial bodies (bodies that might break the protocol, resist framework vocabulary, or produce incoherent scans) have not been systematically tested. A corpus with 100% successful scans is either scanning with a perfect tool or scanning only compatible subjects.

**Conditions under which O>I inverts:** When the corpus is cited as evidence FOR the framework rather than as demonstrations OF the framework's application. Evidence requires independent replication and adversarial testing. Demonstrations show capability but do not prove validity. If the corpus is used to claim the framework is "proven" rather than "demonstrated," it has become marketing material — extracting credibility rather than providing insight.
