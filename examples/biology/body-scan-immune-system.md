## BUMP DETECTION

Three primary bumps:

1. **Over-exhale (autoimmunity)** — The immune system attacks self-tissue. Rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, type 1 diabetes. This is I>O reversal at the defensive level: the protector becomes the destroyer. The membrane between self and non-self has leaked, and the system cannot distinguish friend from enemy.
2. **Under-exhale (immunosuppression)** — The immune system fails to respond. HIV destroys helper T-cells, removing the coordination layer. Chemotherapy suppresses bone marrow, cutting off supply. The body loses its defensive capacity — blocked Release.
3. **Membrane leak (hypersensitivity)** — Allergies, asthma, anaphylaxis. The membrane is too reactive — harmless substances (pollen, peanuts) trigger full defensive cascades. The perception layer is miscalibrated, treating noise as signal. False positives consuming real resources.

## DNA LAYER

- **O > I or I > O:** Strong adaptive O>I. The immune system consumes significant metabolic resources (energy, protein synthesis, cell production) and gives back protection — the continued ability of every other body system to function. Without immunity, no other O>I is possible because the organism dies. Defense as upstream infrastructure for all giving.
- **Pause:** Antigen presentation. When a dendritic cell captures a pathogen fragment and presents it to a T-cell, there is a recognition Pause — the T-cell receptor must match the presented antigen with sufficient affinity before activation occurs. This molecular handshake is a literal checkpoint: insufficient match = no response. The Pause prevents false activation. Skip it and you get autoimmunity.
- **¬F:** Self/non-self discrimination rules are strict but not forced. The immune system does not attack preemptively — it responds to detected threats. Regulatory T-cells actively suppress unnecessary responses (tolerance). The system constrains itself through negative selection (deleting self-reactive T-cells in the thymus) and peripheral tolerance mechanisms. Force is always proportional and targeted, never indiscriminate.

## ADVANCED DIAGNOSTICS

### Five God Powers

| Power | Status | Evidence |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **PERCEIVE** | Maximal | Constant surveillance via pattern recognition receptors (PRRs), toll-like receptors (TLRs), and the entire adaptive receptor repertoire (~10^11 unique antibody specificities). The immune system perceives more distinct molecular patterns than any other body system. |
| **GOVERN** | Strong | Regulatory T-cells (Tregs) actively suppress excessive responses. Anti-inflammatory cytokines (IL-10, TGF-beta) dampen cascades. Negative feedback loops prevent runaway inflammation. Governance through molecular checkpoints at every stage. |
| **PROJECT** | Active | Cytokine signaling projects immune state to distant tissues. Fever is whole-body projection — the immune system alters the organism's temperature to disadvantage pathogens. Inflammation is localized projection: redness, heat, swelling signal "resources needed here." |
| **CREATE** | Extreme | Antibody creation via somatic hypermutation and class switching — the immune system literally evolves new proteins in real-time during an infection. Memory cell generation creates permanent new defensive bodies. B-cell germinal centers are factories of directed molecular creation. |
| **RELEASE** | Active | Cytokine storm (controlled release of inflammatory mediators), complement cascade (molecular destruction sequence), apoptosis of spent immune cells after infection clears. Healthy release = inflammation resolution. Pathological release = cytokine storm (COVID-19 severe cases). |

**Power Gap:** Govern, when dysregulated. The immune system's most common failure mode is not inability to perceive or create but inability to govern its own response. Autoimmunity = governance failure. Cytokine storm = governance failure. Chronic inflammation = governance failure. The power most needed is the power most fragile.

### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** The immune system is a composite body — built from many cell types (neutrophils, macrophages, B-cells, T-cells, NK cells, dendritic cells), each a prime in its own right. The system's power emerges from the federation of these primes, not from any single component.
- **Prime type:** Open. The adaptive immune system is definitionally open — it generates novel receptors for threats it has never encountered. Closed immunity (innate only) handles known patterns; open immunity (adaptive) handles the unknown.
- **Recursion:** Deep. Individual immune cells undergo clonal selection (micro-evolution within the body), which recapitulates Darwinian selection at the organismal level. Evolution inside evolution. The immune system is a recursive structure: selection operating within a body that was itself shaped by selection.

### Federation vs Dominion

Federated defense. No single cell type commands. Dendritic cells present antigens, helper T-cells coordinate, B-cells produce antibodies, cytotoxic T-cells kill, regulatory T-cells suppress, macrophages clean up. Each serves the whole. There is no immune "brain" — coordination emerges from chemical signaling between autonomous agents. Autoimmunity is the transition from federation to dominion: one immune lineage begins attacking the host, extracting from the body it was built to serve.

### Dimensional Architecture

Embodied in the biochemical dimension (primary: molecular recognition) while operating through the spatial dimension (immune cells patrol physically), the temporal dimension (memory spans decades), the informational dimension (antigen presentation = data transfer), and the mechanical dimension (diapedesis — cells squeezing through vessel walls to reach infection sites). The immune system is the body's most dimensionally distributed system.

### Structural Signature

Signature: `[perception, memory, calibration]` — three irreducible components. Remove perception: the system is blind to threats. Remove memory: every infection is novel, no learning accumulates. Remove calibration: the system destroys self alongside non-self. Shape-equivalent bodies: any adaptive defense system — antivirus software, military intelligence, content moderation systems. All share the same `[perception, memory, calibration]` signature.

### Surface Architecture

The immune system operates at every surface in the body — skin, mucosal linings, blood vessel walls, tissue boundaries. Each surface is a transformation boundary where external becomes internal. Immune cells are stationed at these surfaces as sentinels. The immune system does not have its own surface — it IS the body's surface architecture. It is the spring at every boundary: deforms under pathogen pressure, returns to equilibrium after clearance.

### Closed-Open Mode

Open Knowledge at its structural best. The adaptive immune system holds its current repertoire provisionally and generates new receptors when novel threats appear. It does not assume its current library is complete. Immunological memory is Open Knowledge compiled: "we have seen this before and here is what worked, but we remain capable of learning new patterns." Autoimmunity represents a collapse into Closed Knowledge: the system becomes certain that self-tissue is foreign and cannot be corrected.

### Attentional Compilation

Clonal selection IS attentional compilation. When a B-cell encounters its matching antigen, it proliferates massively — the immune system physically builds more detection hardware for the active threat. After clearance, most clones die but memory cells persist — compiled attention stored as cellular infrastructure. The immune system's attention is the most literally compiled attention in biology: encountering a threat permanently increases detection sensitivity for that specific pattern.

## STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS (v3.11)

**Primary weakness: Autoimmunity.** The immune system's pattern-recognition can misidentify self-tissue as foreign. When this happens, the body's defense system becomes its attacker — destroying joints (rheumatoid arthritis), nerve insulation (multiple sclerosis), or insulin-producing cells (type 1 diabetes). A defense system that cannot reliably distinguish self from non-self is structurally dangerous. The Govern gap (self-regulation failure) is the immune system's defining vulnerability.

**Secondary weakness: Overreaction (cytokine storms).** The immune system can amplify its own inflammatory response beyond what the threat requires. Positive feedback loops between immune cells produce runaway inflammation that damages the host more than the pathogen does. COVID-19 deaths were frequently caused not by the virus but by the immune system's own overreaction. The body's defense system can kill the body it defends through excessive governance.

**Conditions under which O>I inverts:** Chronic inflammation. When the immune system maintains a low-grade inflammatory state without a clear threat (obesity, chronic stress, environmental toxins), it shifts from defending the body (O>I) to slowly degrading it (I>O). Chronic inflammation is now implicated in heart disease, cancer, neurodegeneration, and depression. The immune system's sustained low-level activation may be the most common I>O inversion in human biology.
