## BUMP DETECTION

Three primary bumps:

1. **Membrane compromise** — when the plasma membrane loses selectivity (toxin damage, viral insertion), unregulated flow floods the interior. The cell loses sovereignty. This is the cellular analogue of a nation with no border policy.
2. **Replication error** — when DNA polymerase miscopies during S-phase and repair mechanisms fail, the cell's compiled instructions degrade. Accumulated errors = aging. Catastrophic errors = cancer.
3. **Energy blockade** — when mitochondrial function fails (hypoxia, poison), ATP production halts and all downstream processes freeze. The breath stops. Without energy, the cell cannot maintain its membrane, cannot replicate, cannot exhale.

## DNA LAYER

- **O > I or I > O:** Textbook O>I. The cell inhales simple nutrients (glucose, amino acids) and exhales ordered structure — proteins, metabolites, signals, copies of itself. A single cell transforms environmental noise into biological information. Cancer is the pathological inversion: I>O at the cellular level, consuming host resources while producing nothing the organism needs.
- **Pause:** Mitosis checkpoint. Before a cell commits to division, it pauses at G1/S and G2/M boundaries to verify DNA integrity, nutrient sufficiency, and external signals. This is not metaphor — it is a literal molecular Pause where the cell integrates information before acting. Skip the checkpoint, get cancer.
- **¬F:** The genetic code constrains without forcing. DNA sets boundary conditions (what proteins CAN be made) but does not dictate what IS made at any moment. Gene expression is regulated by environment, signals, and epigenetic state. The genome is a possibility space, not a command script.

## ADVANCED DIAGNOSTICS

### Five God Powers

| Power | Status | Evidence |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **PERCEIVE** | Active | Thousands of membrane receptors sense chemical gradients, light, temperature, pressure, neighboring cells. The cell perceives its world continuously. |
| **GOVERN** | Active | Gene regulatory networks control which proteins are expressed. Feedback loops maintain homeostasis. The cell governs its own interior state. |
| **PROJECT** | Active | Cells secrete signaling molecules (cytokines, hormones, neurotransmitters) that alter the behavior of distant cells. Projection through chemistry. |
| **CREATE** | Active | Cell division — the cell creates a complete copy of itself. Also creates novel proteins, metabolites, and structures (cilia, flagella, extracellular matrix). |
| **RELEASE** | Active | Exocytosis, secretion, apoptosis. The cell releases waste, signals, and ultimately itself (programmed cell death as the final release). |

**Power Gap:** None in a healthy cell — all five powers are active. This is precisely WHY the cell is the minimal sovereign unit. A body with all five powers active is self-governing. When any power degrades, pathology follows: loss of Perceive = blindness to signals (cancer ignoring growth-stop signals), loss of Govern = dysregulation, loss of Release = toxic accumulation.

### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** The cell is a prime body — it cannot be decomposed into smaller living units. Organelles (mitochondria, ribosomes) are parts but not independently alive in the current configuration. The cell is the atom of biology: the smallest unit that satisfies all criteria for life.
- **Prime type:** Open. The cell is constantly exchanging with its environment. A closed cell is a dead cell.
- **Recursion:** Deep. Cells contain organelles that were once independent cells (mitochondria = endosymbiosis). The cell is a compiled federation of formerly independent bodies — recursion fossilized into structure.

### Federation vs Dominion

Pure federation internally. Organelles cooperate without a central dictator — the nucleus holds instructions but does not "command" in real-time. Mitochondria generate energy, ribosomes build proteins, lysosomes clean waste. Each serves the whole. The cell's internal economy is O>I at every node. Cancer is the transition from federation to dominion: one cell lineage begins extracting from the organism without serving it.

### Dimensional Architecture

The cell is embodied in the biochemical dimension (primary anchor: chemistry) while operating through the informational dimension (DNA/RNA), the mechanical dimension (cytoskeleton, motility), the electrical dimension (membrane potential), and the temporal dimension (cell cycle, circadian rhythms). It is a bridge body spanning at least five dimensions through a single membrane.

### Structural Signature

Signature: `[membrane, genome, metabolism]` — three irreducible components. Remove any one and you no longer have a living cell. Membrane without genome = vesicle (not alive). Genome without membrane = naked DNA (not sovereign). Metabolism without genome = chemistry (not replicable). Shape-equivalent bodies: any minimal self-replicating unit — the theoretical "protocell" at the origin of life.

### Surface Architecture

The plasma membrane IS the primary surface — the transformation boundary where external chemistry becomes internal biology. Nutrients cross inward and become substrate; waste crosses outward and becomes exhaust. The membrane is a spring surface: it deforms under osmotic pressure but returns to equilibrium. Every receptor on the membrane is a micro-surface where an external signal flips into an internal cascade.

### Closed-Open Mode

Open Knowledge. The cell "knows" its environment through continuous sensing and updates its behavior accordingly (gene expression changes in response to signals). It holds no model as final — a liver cell can be reprogrammed into a stem cell given sufficient signal. Epigenetic state is provisional, not permanent. The cell is the biological embodiment of Open Knowledge: responsive, updatable, non-dogmatic.

### Attentional Compilation

The cell's attention operates through receptor density — more receptors for a given signal means higher attentional resolution for that signal. A photoreceptor cell has compiled extreme attention to light; a macrophage has compiled extreme attention to foreign antigens. Attentional compilation is literal in biology: the cell physically builds more detection hardware for what matters. Sniper scope, not floodlight — each cell type attends to a narrow band with extraordinary precision.
