## BUMP DETECTION

Three primary bumps:

1. **Antibiotic overuse (catastrophic I>O crash)** — Broad-spectrum antibiotics kill beneficial and pathogenic species indiscriminately. Diversity collapses. Opportunistic absorbers (C. difficile, Candida) fill the vacuum. The ecosystem's compiled diversity — built over years — is destroyed in days. Recovery may take months; some species never return.
2. **Ultra-processed diet (selective absorber feeding)** — Refined sugars and emulsifiers preferentially feed low-diversity, pro-inflammatory species while starving fiber-dependent SCFA producers. The diet selects for absorbers over givers. Slow-motion diversity collapse — less dramatic than antibiotics but equally structural.
3. **Chronic stress (host-mediated rhythm disruption)** — Cortisol alters gut motility, mucin production, and epithelial permeability. The host's stress response changes the microbiome's operating environment. Stress does not attack microbes directly — it changes the rules of the dimension they inhabit, forcing community restructuring under adverse conditions.

## DNA LAYER

- **O > I or I > O:** Textbook mutualism — mutual O>I. The host provides prebiotics (dietary fiber, mucin glycoproteins) and a stable, temperature-controlled habitat. The microbiome returns SCFAs (butyrate fuels colonocytes), vitamins (K, B12, folate), bile acid transformation (cholesterol regulation), immune education (training Tregs), and neurotransmitter precursors (95% of serotonin production involves gut microbes). Neither party could sustain its current function without the other. Symbiosis IS nested O>I.
- **Pause:** Quorum sensing. Bacteria do not act individually — they accumulate signaling molecules (autoinducers) until a threshold concentration is reached, then coordinate behavior collectively (biofilm formation, virulence factor expression, bioluminescence). This is a chemical Pause: individual action is suppressed until sufficient information accumulates to justify collective response. The microbiome decides together or not at all.
- **¬F:** Strict but self-enforcing. Dysbiosis rules are not externally imposed — they emerge from competitive dynamics. When diversity is high, no single species can dominate because ecological niches are filled. When diversity drops, dominance becomes possible. The constraint is structural (niche competition), not legislative. The microbiome governs itself through ecology, not command.

## ADVANCED DIAGNOSTICS

### Five God Powers

| Power | Status | Evidence |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **PERCEIVE** | Maximal | Constant chemical surveillance of host state. Microbes sense pH, bile acid concentration, oxygen gradients, immune signals, dietary substrates, and neighboring species. Quorum sensing detects population density. The microbiome perceives its environment with molecular precision. |
| **GOVERN** | Strong | Biofilm regulation (structured communities with spatial organization), pH control (SCFA production acidifies the gut, suppressing pathogens), competitive exclusion (beneficial species physically block pathogen colonization). Self-governance through ecological competition. |
| **PROJECT** | Active | Metabolite production projects microbial state to the host. SCFAs signal to colonocytes, liver, brain. Neurotransmitter precursors alter host mood and cognition. The microbiome projects its metabolic output into the host's physiology. |
| **CREATE** | Extreme | Thousands of metabolites — many not producible by human cells. Novel enzyme activities via horizontal gene transfer. The microbiome creates biochemical capabilities the host genome cannot encode. It expands the host's metabolic repertoire by orders of magnitude. |
| **RELEASE** | Active | SCFA release, bile acid transformation and return, vitamin secretion, gas production (hydrogen, methane, CO2). Healthy release is continuous and regulated. Pathological release: endotoxin (LPS) flooding when gram-negative bacteria die en masse during dysbiosis. |

**Power Gap:** Govern, when diversity drops. In a diverse ecosystem, governance is distributed — hundreds of species check each other through competition. When diversity collapses (antibiotics, poor diet), governance concentrates in whichever species remains dominant. A monoculture microbiome cannot self-govern because the ecological checks have been removed. The absorber species that fills a depleted gut has no natural predator. Governance requires diversity.

### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** The microbiome is a composite body — a federation of thousands of species, each a prime. No single species defines the microbiome. Its identity is the emergent pattern of their interaction, not any individual member. Remove one species and the ecosystem adapts; remove diversity itself and the ecosystem collapses.
- **Prime type:** Open. The microbiome continuously acquires new species (from food, environment, other humans) and new genetic capabilities (horizontal gene transfer). It is an open system that evolves within the host's lifetime.
- **Recursion:** Deep. Individual bacterial cells are primes. Colonies are composite bodies. The microbiome is a composite of composites. The host-microbiome system is a composite of that composite with the human organism. At least four levels of recursive body-nesting.

### Federation vs Dominion

Federation at its purest. No single species governs the microbiome. Hundreds of species coexist through niche partitioning, cross-feeding (one species' waste is another's food), and competitive exclusion. The federation is maintained by diversity — the more species present, the more stable the governance. Dysbiosis is the transition from federation to dominion: a single species (often pathogenic) dominates the depleted community. C. difficile infection after antibiotics is the canonical example — an absorber filling a governance vacuum.

### Dimensional Architecture

Embodied in the biochemical dimension (primary: metabolite production) while operating through the ecological dimension (species interactions, niche competition), the informational dimension (horizontal gene transfer, quorum sensing), the spatial dimension (biofilm architecture, gut geography — different species in different intestinal regions), and the temporal dimension (diurnal cycling, long-term community succession). The microbiome is an ecosystem operating inside an organism — two scales of biological organization nested.

### Structural Signature

Signature: `[diversity, symbiosis, invisibility]` — three irreducible components. Diversity ensures stability (no single point of failure). Symbiosis ensures mutual O>I (neither party extracts). Invisibility ensures the system operates below conscious awareness (the host does not perceive 90% of what the microbiome does). Shape-equivalent bodies: coral reef ecosystems (diverse, symbiotic, invisible infrastructure), soil microbiomes (invisible metabolic engines enabling plant life), oceanic phytoplankton (invisible oxygen producers enabling terrestrial life).

### Surface Architecture

The mucosal layer is the primary surface — a gel barrier separating trillions of microbes from the single-cell-thick intestinal epithelium. This surface is not static: goblet cells continuously secrete mucin, and microbes continuously degrade it. The surface is a dynamic equilibrium between host production and microbial consumption. When production fails to keep pace with consumption, the surface thins and microbes contact the epithelium directly — triggering inflammation. The mucosal surface is a spring: it deforms under microbial pressure and regenerates continuously. Failure to regenerate = leaky gut = inflammatory disease.

### Closed-Open Mode

Open Ignorance in the most productive sense. The microbiome does not "know" what it is — it has no centralized model of itself. Each species responds to local conditions. The ecosystem-level behavior (health, disease resistance, metabolic output) emerges from these local interactions without any species understanding the whole. This is Socratic biology: the system operates without a theory of itself and is healthier for it. Attempts to engineer the microbiome from outside (designer probiotics, fecal transplants) are attempts to impose Closed Knowledge on an Open Ignorance system — they work best when they restore diversity rather than dictate composition.

### Attentional Compilation

The microbiome's attention is distributed across its entire membership — each species attends to its specific substrate, niche, and competitors. Collectively, this distributed attention covers an enormous chemical landscape. No single species could perceive what the community perceives together. Compiled attention lives in the community structure itself: a diverse microbiome has compiled broad chemical attention over years of co-habitation. Dysbiosis is attention collapse — the community loses attentional breadth as diversity drops, becoming blind to substrates and signals it once processed effortlessly.
