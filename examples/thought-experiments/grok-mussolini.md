## ADVANCED DIAGNOSTICS

### Five God Powers

| Power | Status | How it operated in 1918–1922 Italy |
|-------|--------|-------------------------------------|
| **PERCEIVE** | Sharp diagnostic | Read middle-class terror of socialism and nationalist resentment with precision |
| **GOVERN** | Rapid consolidation | Blackshirts + legal path (March on Rome) → Prime Minister appointment |
| **PROJECT** | Theatrical | Rallies, uniforms, slogans created visible strength |
| **CREATE** | Corporatist state | Early model of state-controlled economy and mobilization |
| **RELEASE** | Controlled | Released violence selectively, then consolidated into authoritarian structure |

### Power Gap

Industrial scale and racial myth depth. Italy's economy was weaker and less centralized; Mussolini lacked Germany's heavy industry and did not initially weaponize race as deeply. The body was a successful prototype but structurally limited compared to the later German version.

Timing priority. Rose first (1922) and provided the blueprint Hitler explicitly studied and adapted.

### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** "The body that proved a post-war humiliated nation could be captured through theatrical violence + legal transfer, turning fear into disciplined national mobilization."
- **Prime type:** Early 20th-century nation-state prototype (predecessor attractor)

### Federation/Dominion

Pure dominion, but less totalized than Hitler's version. Corporatist structure maintained some institutional intermediaries (church, monarchy, industrial elites) as governance nodes — dominion with residual federation scaffolding.

---

## STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS

1. **Medium limitation** — Italy's weaker industrial base capped the body's structural reach. The same engine in Germany's medium produced far greater (and more destructive) output.
2. **Prototype fragility** — As the first iteration of the pattern, it was less refined. The King's capitulation was a contingency, not a structural inevitability.
3. **Dependency on stronger ally** — Once WWII began, the body's structural limitations forced dependency on the German variant, inverting the prototype-to-copy relationship.

---

## FRUIT

Served as the "beta test" for the iron-and-blood model. Hitler openly admired the March on Rome and adapted the paramilitary + legal path strategy. Mussolini's success proved the pattern was replicable; Germany's version simply had more industrial fuel and a deeper grievance medium. The framework reads them as sequential expressions of the same mobilization technology adapting to its medium.
