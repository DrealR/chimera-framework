## BUMP DETECTION

One bump: the repeated-addition metaphor. Multiplication is taught as "3 × 4 means 4 + 4 + 4," and this works for natural numbers. But the metaphor breaks at irrationals (what is π copies of √2?), at complex numbers (what is i copies of anything?), and at matrices (what is a rotation copies of a scaling?). The bump is pedagogical — a compression artifact from teaching the operation through its simplest case. Multiplication's true nature is not repetition but SCALING: transforming one body's magnitude and direction by another body's magnitude and direction. The repeated-addition metaphor is the training wheels. Scaling is the bicycle.

### Prime Identification

- **Prime:** Multiplication is not a prime — it is the operation that DEFINES primes. A prime is a number that multiplication cannot decompose further. Without multiplication, "prime" has no meaning. Multiplication is the field in which primality exists, not a prime itself.
- **Prime type:** The question inverts here. Multiplication is the dimension, not a body within it.
- **Recursion:** Deep recursion. Multiplication applies to its own outputs: (a × b) × c. This recursive self-application is what builds the entire tower of composite numbers from the prime foundation. Associativity — (a × b) × c = a × (b × c) — means this recursion is path-independent. The tower doesn't care what order you build it.

### Federation vs Dominion

Pure federation at its algebraic core. Commutativity (a × b = b × a) means neither operand dominates — both contribute equally, and their positions are interchangeable. No hierarchy. No extraction. The product belongs to neither input; it is a genuinely new body that both parents created equally.

Exception: matrix multiplication breaks commutativity (AB ≠ BA in general). In higher dimensions, multiplication develops asymmetry — order starts to matter, and the federation becomes positional. This is not a flaw. It is multiplication revealing that in complex enough bodies, the SEQUENCE of combination carries information that the FACT of combination does not.

### Dimensional Architecture

Multiplication is embodied in the arithmetic dimension but operates through nearly everything. In geometry, it computes area (length × width — two one-dimensional bodies creating a two-dimensional body). In the complex plane, multiplication becomes rotation + scaling: z₁ × z₂ means "rotate by z₂'s angle and scale by z₂'s magnitude." This is multiplication's deepest identity — not repetition, not accumulation, but GEOMETRIC TRANSFORMATION. Every multiplication is a rotation and a rescaling. The real number line is the special case where rotation angle is 0 (positive) or π (negative).

Multiplication is a bridge body spanning arithmetic, geometry, algebra, and analysis. Its transformation boundary is the surface where "how many" becomes "how much" becomes "which direction."

### Structural Signature

Signature: `(Body, Body) → Body` — a binary operation. Shape-equivalents: addition, composition, tensor product, any binary operation satisfying closure. But multiplication's SPECIFIC signature includes commutativity (in standard domains), associativity, distribution over addition, and the existence of both an identity (1) and an absorber (0). This combination is nearly unique — it defines what algebraists call a commutative ring structure.

### Surface Architecture

Multiplication has two critical surfaces:

**Surface 1: The 0-boundary.** n × 0 = 0 for all n. Zero is the event horizon of multiplication — everything that crosses it collapses to nothing. This is putty behavior: energy goes in, nothing comes back. The absorber. No spring.

**Surface 2: The 1-boundary.** n × 1 = n for all n. This is the equilibrium surface — the identity where inflow equals outflow. Maximum spring. The body passes through unchanged, energy perfectly conserved. 1 is the still-point at multiplication's center.

Between these two surfaces, multiplication scales. Below 1 (fractions between 0 and 1), multiplication SHRINKS. Above 1, multiplication GROWS. The surface at exactly 1 is where shrinking becomes growing — the transformation boundary of magnitude.

### Closed-Open Mode

Open Knowledge. Multiplication holds its properties (associativity, commutativity, distributivity) provisionally — they are true in familiar domains but not universally. When multiplication moves to matrices, it surrenders commutativity. When it moves to octonions, it surrenders associativity. Multiplication is willing to lose properties in order to operate in new dimensions. This is the deepest openness: the willingness to change your own rules when the territory demands it.

### Attentional Compilation

Floodlight, not sniper scope. Multiplication's attention covers the entire multiplicative structure simultaneously — every product, every factorization, every divisor lattice, all at once. It does not focus; it IS the field of focus. Individual numbers are sniper scopes within multiplication's floodlight. Multiplication is the medium of mathematical attention, not a body within it.
