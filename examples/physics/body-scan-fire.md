## STRUCTURAL WEAKNESS

1. **Containment dependency** — Its biggest gap. Fire is only useful when relationally held. Without external governance (hearth, furnace, engine cylinder), it destroys rather than serves.
2. **Fuel-to-energy translation specificity** — Fire translates stored chemical bonds (fuel) into heat and light (energy) through oxidation (rapid molecular disassembly), at the cost of irreversibility — the fuel is permanently consumed and cannot be reconstituted. The transformation is one-directional: wood becomes ash, oil becomes exhaust, hydrogen becomes water. This irreversibility is both its power (entropy increase drives the energy release) and its structural limit (it cannot un-burn).
3. **Feedback sensitivity** — Small changes in fuel or oxygen create massive state shifts (ember to wildfire). The body is a nonlinear amplifier with no internal damping.
4. **Pathological twin** — Uncontained fire is the healthy version of cancer's logic: replication without regard for the larger system.
