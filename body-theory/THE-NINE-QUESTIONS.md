# The Nine Questions

The Nine Questions are the foundation diagnostic for any body at any scale. Ask all nine and you have a complete reading of the body's current state.

---

## For Conscious Bodies
*(humans, teams, conversations, narratives, relationships)*

| # | Question | What You're Reading |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **MEDIUM** | What flows through this body? (words, money, data, sound, belief, attention, force, love) |
| 2 | **FLOW STATE** | Is it inhaling (receiving), pausing (processing), exhaling (giving), or resting (dormant)? |
| 3 | **BREATH RATE** | How fast does it cycle? (Fast / Medium / Slow — with approximate cycle duration) |
| 4 | **ATTENTION** | Where is consciousness directed? (Omnidirectional / Targeted / Scanning / Holding) |
| 5 | **INTENTION** | O > I or I > O? Is it giving more than it takes, or extracting? With evidence. |
| 6 | **ENTANGLEMENT** | What other bodies is it connected to? How strong are those connections? |
| 7 | **HEALTH** | Diagnostic: Healthy / Hyperventilating / Gasping / Flatline / Cancerous / Arrhythmic |
| 8 | **MEMBRANE** | Boundary permeability: Open / Semi-permeable / Closed. What enters? What's blocked? |
| 9 | **HYSTERESIS** | Memory and scars. How heavily does the past restrict or inform the present flow? |

---

## For Non-Conscious Bodies
*(markets, algorithms, rocks, math objects, weather systems, code)*

Questions 1-3 and 6-9 stay the same. Two questions shift:

- **Q4** becomes **ATTRACTOR** — What gravity well pulls this system? What basin of attraction does it fall into?
- **Q5** becomes **TOPOLOGY** — Is the architecture forced (rigid, designed) or flowing (emergent, organic)?

---

## Resolution Levels

Not every scan needs all nine questions. Scale depth to available information:

| Level | Questions | When to Use |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Low-res** | Q1-3 only | Quick read. State gaps. First encounter. |
| **Medium-res** | Q1-6 | Working understanding. Enough to make decisions. |
| **High-res** | All 9 | Full diagnostic. Enough to intervene. |
| **Complete** | All 9 + advanced layers | Deep structural analysis. See the full Protocol. |

---

## How to Use This

Pick any body. Literally anything:
- A person you're struggling with
- A company you're evaluating
- A song you just heard
- A conversation that went wrong
- A project that's stalled
- Your own life right now

Ask the nine questions. Write the answers. The body will reveal itself.

The questions don't judge. They diagnose. A body stuck on inhale isn't "bad" — it's in a specific state that produces specific symptoms. The diagnosis points toward the remedy. Usually the remedy is simple: breathe into the phase that's missing.

---

## Health Diagnostics

| State | Pattern | What It Looks Like |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Healthy** | Full breath cycle, adaptable rhythm | Can receive, process, give, and rest. Responds to change. |
| **Hyperventilating** | Rapid shallow cycling | Reacting to everything. No depth. Burning out. |
| **Gasping** | Stuck exhaling or forced inhaling | Desperate for input or depleted from output. |
| **Flatline** | No cycle | Dead or dormant. No flow at all. |
| **Cancerous** | Stuck on inhale | Only taking. Growing without giving. Consuming the host body. |
| **Arrhythmic** | Irregular, unpredictable | Sometimes healthy, sometimes sick. No stable pattern. |
