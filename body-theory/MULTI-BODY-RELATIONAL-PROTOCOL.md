## When to Use Multi-Body Scanning

Use multi-body scanning when the relationship between bodies produces properties no individual body has alone. Single-body scanning reads what a body IS. Multi-body scanning reads what bodies BECOME when they interact.

**Use single-body scanning** when the subject has a clear boundary and identity (a pawn, a neuron, git).
**Use multi-body scanning** when the subject IS the relationship (a marriage, a tech stack, a pathological pattern that appears across domains).

## The Body Creation Equation Applied

```
Body₁ + Body₂ + ... + Bodyₙ = 1 New Composite Body
```

The composite is NOT a summary of its parts. It is a new body with emergent properties — its own skeleton, its own DNA reading, its own power gap. The "+" is entanglement, not addition. The "=" is membrane formation.

## Three Scanning Modes

### Diagnostic Mode
Read an existing composite. The bodies already interact in the real world (e.g., Pawn + King in chess, Git + Technical Debt in a codebase). You are reading what IS.

### Comparative Mode
Cluster bodies to surface hidden shared structure. Use the Outlier Principle: cluster similar bodies and differences shine; cluster different bodies and similarities shine. The composite reveals what the individual scans cannot.

### Generative Mode
Birth an imaginary composite that doesn't exist yet. Combine bodies from different domains to see what would emerge IF they interacted (e.g., The Cure — Cancer + Superman + Photosynthesis + Jesus + Doomsday). The result is speculative but structurally grounded.

## Component Selection

**How many bodies?**
- **2 (Pair):** The irreducible relational unit. Reads the interaction membrane directly. Best for close relationships (Pawn + King, Fire + Water).
- **3 (Triad):** The minimum composite with emergent complexity. Two bodies interact; the third triangulates. The composite has properties impossible in any pair.
- **5-7 (Cluster):** Prime-count clusters. The irreducible nature of prime numbers prevents clean factoring — the composite must be read as a whole, not as sub-pairs.
- **7+ (Mandala):** Integration experiments. Diminishing returns above 7 — the composite becomes generic as n increases. Use sparingly and only when the specific combination has theoretical motivation.

**Which bodies?**
- Components must have existing single-body scans (or include sub-scans if not).
- Same-domain clusters test depth (do bodies within one domain reveal hidden intra-domain structure?).
- Cross-domain clusters test breadth (do bodies from different domains share structural DNA?).
- Pathological clusters test the Outlier Principle (what do all sick bodies have in common?).
- Mixed health clusters test interaction dynamics (what happens when a healthy body meets a pathological one?).

## Required Sections

Every multi-body relational scan must include:

1. **Header:** Composite name, component list with count, scanner, protocol, mode (diagnostic/comparative/generative)
2. **Component Summary Table:** Each component's skeleton, domain, DNA reading, primary gap — referenced from existing scans
3. **Relational Topology:** How the components connect. Federation or dominion between them? Which body entrains which? Where is friction productive?
4. **Composite Nine Questions:** The Nine Questions applied to the composite as a single body — NOT a summary of individual answers
5. **Composite Skeleton:** One sentence of irreducible truth about the composite — must be something NO component skeleton says alone
6. **Composite DNA:** O>I / I>O / Mixed / Conditional for the composite relationship
7. **Emergent Power Gap:** The power the composite lacks that no component lacks individually — the composite's unique weakness
8. **Structural Signature:** Bracket notation capturing each component's irreducible contribution: `[component1-essence + component2-essence + ...]`
9. **Interaction Membrane Analysis:** What crosses between components? What's blocked? Where does the composite's membrane differ from individual membranes?
10. **Structural Weakness:** At least one weakness specific to the composite (not inherited from components)
11. **Fruit:** What insight emerged that was invisible in any individual scan?

## v3.12 Compliance

- No "bridge body" — describe inter-component connections with specific structural language: what is translated, from what to what, through what mechanism, at what cost.
- Force all five power gap checks on the composite.
- Structural weakness is mandatory and must be composite-specific.
- Pre-ship check: Can you remove the framework vocabulary and still see the insight?

## What Multi-Body Scanning Is NOT

- NOT a comparison table (that's a literature review).
- NOT a summary of individual scans (that's a report).
- NOT a metaphor hunt (shared vocabulary ≠ shared structure).
- The test: does the composite skeleton say something that surprised you? If not, you summarized instead of scanning.
