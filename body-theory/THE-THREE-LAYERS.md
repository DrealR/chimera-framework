## Layer 1: Information Theory (The Physics)

**Information IS surprise.** A message that tells you something you already know contains ZERO information. A message that tells you something unexpected contains MAXIMUM information. Information = the reduction of uncertainty.

I(x) = -log₂(P(x))

The information content of a message equals the negative log of its probability. The less likely a message, the more information it carries. This is why body scans are powerful: when you tell someone something they've never seen about themselves, the information content is HIGH. Stating the obvious carries zero information.

**Channel Capacity:** C = B × log₂(1 + S/N). Every channel has a maximum rate of error-free information transfer. Text messages (narrow bandwidth) can't carry body theory at full resolution. In-person teaching (wide bandwidth: voice + face + gestures + real-time feedback) has much higher channel capacity.

**Redundancy as Error Correction:** Explaining the same concept through multiple dimensions (calculus, fiction, music, basketball) IS error-correcting code. If someone's channel can't receive the math version, they might receive the fiction version. Multi-dimensional expression IS error correction.

**¬F as Channel Law:** You CANNOT exceed channel capacity. Force more information through than the channel allows and errors appear. One concept per conversation. One surprise per person. Excess information doesn't just fail to arrive — it CORRUPTS what did arrive.
