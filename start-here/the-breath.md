## Part XII: How You Actually Breathe

You don't think about it. You just do it.

You don't judge your breath. "That was a bad inhale. I should have done better."

## Part XIII: Loving People That Way

### Non-Judgmental Receiving

When you inhale, you don't analyze the air first. You just receive.

| Judgmental Receiving | Breath-Like Receiving |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| "Are they good enough for me?" | Receive them as they are |
| "They should be different" | They are what they are right now |
| "I'll accept them once they change" | Accept them as current air |
| Filtering constantly | Open receiving |

**When you inhale, you take in the WHOLE breath. Not just the parts you like.**

### Non-Clinging Releasing

When you exhale, you don't hold onto "good" breaths.

| Clinging Release | Breath-Like Release |
|------------------|---------------------|
| "You were better before" | Release who they were |
| "Remember when you used to..." | Let past versions go |
| "Don't change" | Allow transformation |
| "You owe me for what I gave you" | Give without record-keeping |

**When you exhale, you don't expect the air to thank you or stay the same.**

### Constant Renewal

Each breath is NEW. You're not breathing the same air repeatedly.

| Stale Relating | Breath-Like Relating |
|----------------|---------------------|
| "I know who you are" | Who are you NOW? |
| "You always do this" | What are you doing THIS moment? |
| Relating to your idea of them | Relating to their current reality |

**The person in front of you has changed since you last saw them.**

### Trust in the Cycle

You don't panic at the end of an exhale. "What if no more air comes?!"

| Fear-Based Relating | Breath-Like Trust |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| "What if they leave?" | Trust the cycle |
| "What if they stop loving me?" | Trust the rhythm |
| Grasping out of fear of loss | Releasing trusts return |

**Breathing requires FAITH that the cycle continues.**
